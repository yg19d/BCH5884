Siroheme and its biosynthetic pathway

Siroheme is an iron-containing isobacteriochlorin that serves as a prosthetic group in SiR. It is synthesized by siroheme synthase through a three-step biosynthetic pathway from the precursor uroporphyrinogen III (uro'gen III). The three steps are methylation of uro'gen III to form precorrin-2, dehydrogenation to form sirohydrochlorin, and ferrochelation to form siroheme.

Siroheme synthesis in different organisms

In different organisms, siroheme is synthesized by different enzymes, although the functions remain the same. Some enzymes perform dehydrogenation and ferrochelation, both, and thus are bifunctional while some are monofunctional which carry out one function only.
